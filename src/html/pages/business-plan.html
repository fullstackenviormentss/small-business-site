About business plans

A business plan is a roadmap that will help you prepare for the next three-to-five years of your venture. Before you can start writing, you’ll need to understand the ins-and-outs of your customers, competitors, and community.

Use Census data for your plan

We have a wealth of information that you can use to create a data-driven plan for your business.

Find your classification codes

The North American Identification Classification System (NAICS) is used by the United States, Canada, and Mexico to classify businesses. Identifying your code will help you clearly define your industry.

Conduct market research

Identify market patterns to find opportunities and business risks. Data can help you find out more about your community’s employment, like how many people are employed and how they’re paid by similar businesses. You can also gather information about how potential customers spend their money with consumer spending data. Understanding your market will set a strong foundation for your business plan.

Understand the competition

There are similar businesses in every community, so understanding your competition will help your business thrive. Discover how many industry businesses exist in your neighborhood, or learn more about their product lines and how well they’re selling. Our data will help you create a plan that accounts for the competition.

Identify your customers

Customers are the key to a successful businesses. Whether you’re creating a family-friendly business or looking to cater to a certain cultural group, we have the resources you need to learn about potential customers in your community. Find information about the your potential customers’ ages, median incomes, employment statuses, and more. This data will allow you to build a plan that considers your consumers.

How to write a business plan

Now that you know what data exists, it’s time to write a business plan. Don’t know where to begin? These resources can help you apply Census data to your plan.

Census Business Builder

The Census Business Builder (CBB) allows you to compile data into a report, which can be used to supplement a business plan. Learn how to create a report in CBB.

Small Business Administration

The Small Business Administration (SBA) offers a guide on how to write a business plan. These instructions will teach you how to write an executive summary, or conduct data-driven market analysis. If you’re still stuck, the SBA provides a learning center, as well as local assistance in your community.

SCORE.org

SCORE is a nonprofit association that helps small businesses get off the ground. They provide a template and worksheets to help you write and execute your business plan.

BPlans

BPlans is a free resource to help entrepreneurs start and run better businesses. They offer several resources, including a free SBA-approved course, over 500 free examples, and a template.
